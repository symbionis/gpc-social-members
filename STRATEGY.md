# Geneva Polo Club Social Member Club Strategy

## Target problem

The club fills a room but can't convert it. Flagship events draw big crowds — hundreds
register for a single party — yet almost none become members. A guest comes once, nothing
pulls them back, and a great event produces a mailing list instead of members. The value
leaks out at the door.

## Our approach

Make every event a membership on-ramp: treat the door as the top of the funnel, so every
guest who scans a ticket is captured, followed up, and walked toward joining. The bet is
that conversion happens in the days around an event — not on a website in the abstract.

## Who it's for

**Primary:** The club / admin — hiring the platform to capture and convert the crowd:
run events, capture every guest at the door, follow up, and turn them into members and dues,
without it living in spreadsheets and WhatsApp.

**Secondary:** The prospective member (the guest at an event) — going from "I had a great
night" to "I'm in" with as little friction as possible.

## Key metrics

- **Guest→member conversion rate** — of unique guests who attend an event, the % who become
  members within ~60 days. The core scoreboard for the whole approach. _Source: Supabase._
- **Event check-in rate** — checked-in ÷ registered, per event. You can't convert a guest
  you never captured at the door. _Source: Supabase._
- **New members approved / month** — net membership growth, the lagging outcome. _Source: Supabase._
- **Ticket revenue per event** — CHF collected ÷ event; ties the on-ramp to money and new
  revenue. _Source: Supabase._

_Watch later (not a current focus): member renewal rate — promote to a key metric when
membership lifecycle becomes an investment area._

## Tracks

### Door & Capture

Check-in, rosters, QR tickets, and the guest data captured at the event — plus the ticket
sales that happen there. The top of the on-ramp.

_Why it serves the approach:_ You can't convert a guest you never captured; this is where
the funnel begins and where event revenue lands.

### Conversion & Follow-up

Everything that turns a captured guest into a member: post-event nudges, the apply/join
flow, the membership pitch, and referrals/originators.

_Why it serves the approach:_ This is the literal heart of "every event is an on-ramp" —
the step that turns a crowd into members.

### Member Portal Value

The between-events reason to log in — the value that makes a membership feel worth keeping
once someone has joined.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Conversion doesn't stick if there's nothing behind the door;
this keeps converted members engaged.
